I am currently a 5th year PhD candidate in the Center for Biophysics & Quantitative Biology at the University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign (UIUC) where I work in the [Shukla group](https://shuklagroup.org/). Prior to attenting UIUC I completed my Bachelor of Science in Biochemistry at Virginia Tech where I worked in the [Lemkul lab](https://www.thelemkullab.com/) as an undergraduate researcher.

My research interests center on **ML engineering**, **data science**, and **scientific computing** for problems where large, noisy, and sparse datasets require robust predictive models. I develop machine learning workflows that combine transformer embeddings, graph-based representations, tabular features, ranking models, and generative approaches to support prediction, screening, and optimization at scale. My previous work has focused on computational chemistry and biophysics, including ligand prediction and synthetic data generation for data-limited biological systems such as plant proteins. More broadly, I am interested in building practical ML systems that integrate domain knowledge, scalable data pipelines, rigorous model evaluation, and interpretable outputs to solve real-world problems across scientific and industry applications. 

Outside of my doctoral research, I am also actively working on **two-tower models** and recommendation workflows for large-scale recommendation engines. This work includes building scalable preprocessing pipelines, engineering user and item features, evaluating top-k ranking performance, and integrating model outputs into practical recommendation systems. The system is implemented as a production-oriented ML workflow with **Dockerized services**, **unit-tested components**, **CI/CD practices**, and **Kubernetes-based deployment** for connecting improved model versions to a live web application.
