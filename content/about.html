About Me

My name is Tanner Dean, and I am a third-year PhD student in the Shukla Group. My primary work in the lab focuses on the study of halogenases. Halogenases are enzymes involved in the regioselective halogenation of substrates and many are inolved in natural product synthesis. We are interested in applying methods in machine learning and free energy calculations to modify these enzymes to catalyze reactions of interest to the pharmaceutical and agropharmaceutical industries. I completed my BS in Biochemistry with a minor in Chemistry at Virginia Tech, where I conducted undergraduate research in the Lemukl Lab. My research focused on the study of ions and the effects of polarization on G-Quadruplexes using the Drude polarizable force field.
