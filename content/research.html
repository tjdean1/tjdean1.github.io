Research Interests

Active Learning for Optimized Alchemical Free Energy Calculations

Free energy calculations for predicting binding free energies via molecular dynamics simulations continue to play an important role in the drug development process. One of several key issues in applying these methods is the size of the chemical search space for potential drugs. By developing and applying state of the art schemes in active learning I am interested in reducing the computational efforts needed to explore this vast search space.

Applications of Deep Learning and Large Language Models to Enzyme Development and Modification

While prior work on enzyme design has focused on directed evolution and large screening methods, more recent developments in machine learning such as AlphaFold, ESMFold, RoseTTAFold, and many others have lead to great interest in applying modern computational techniques to rational enzyme design to greatly reduce the search space for mutants of interest.
